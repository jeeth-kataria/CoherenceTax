     Paper Potential — Honest Evaluation

     The Coherence Tax

     What it claims:
     Alignment jointly degrades discourse coherence and epistemic commitment,
     and these two degradations are correlated because they share a mechanism
     (suppression of propositional uncertainty). Tested via 7 model checkpoints,
      200 prompts, 14 features, Wilcoxon + OLS.

     Novel? Partially. "Alignment shifts output style" is crowded (2023–2025
     papers: Shi et al., Sun et al., Ouyang effects, InstructGPT analysis). The
     specific cross-paper integration (CoherenceBench-IN × AlignTax correlation)
      is novel if the Spearman result holds. The Zephyr 3-stage decomposition
     (SFT vs DPO marginal) is genuinely clean and rare.

     Measurable contribution?
     - If Spearman ρ is significant: yes, strong finding.
     - If coherence degrades after length control (OLS): yes, structural not
     verbal.
     - If DPO causes more degradation than SFT: yes, actionable for
     practitioners.

     Paper claim (if results hold):
     "We show that alignment causes statistically significant coherence
     degradation across 3 model families, and that coherence loss and epistemic
     suppression are correlated (ρ ≈ -0.45 to -0.60), suggesting a shared
     suppression mechanism rather than independent effects."

     What could kill it:
     - Null result (coherence not significantly worse post-alignment)
     - Base models generating degenerate text (very possible without instruction
      formatting)
     - Spearman correlation is weak (ρ < -0.30) — hard to claim shared mechanism
     - Marker-based coherence features (regex on 12 words) don't capture real
     discourse structure
     - No human evaluation = reviewers will push back

     Venue fit:
     ACL Findings, EMNLP Findings, or a dedicated workshop (BlackboxNLP,
     RepL4NLP). NOT main ACL without human eval.

     ---
     VisualCOT-Swap

     What it claims:
     VLMs doing Chain-of-Thought on visual questions progressively "drift" from
     the image — early steps are visually grounded, later steps are hallucinated
      reasoning. Measured via Grounding Faithfulness Score (GFS) = spatial
     concentration of attribution heatmap per CoT step. Confirmed by swap-pair
     test: if swapping the image doesn't change reasoning steps, model was never
      using it.

     Novel? Yes. This is the right question at the right time:
     - Multimodal CoT is an active research frontier (LLaVA-CoT, InternVL2 CoT
     papers)
     - Attribution methods applied to multi-step visual reasoning is mostly
     unexplored
     - The swap-pair validity check is methodologically sound and clever
     - Three independent attribution methods (Grad-CAM, rollout, occlusion) =
     robust finding

     Measurable contribution?
     - GFS decay curve with step k = visualizable, citable, replicable
     - Swap sensitivity score = quantifies grounding failure
     - Cross-model comparison = practitioners can choose better-grounded VLM

     Paper claim:
     "We introduce GFS, a step-wise measure of visual grounding faithfulness in
     VLM CoT reasoning. Across 3 VLMs and 200 image-question pairs, GFS decays
     significantly with CoT step depth (mean slope = X, p < 0.05), and swap
     sensitivity reveals that [N%] of reasoning steps beyond step 3 are visually
      ungrounded."

     What could kill it:
     - GFS decay is uniform across all models (no differential finding)
     - Attribution methods (Grad-CAM on 4-bit quantized models) produce noise,
     not signal
     - Swap pairs fail construction (< 60% valid swaps due to same-answer
     constraint)
     - Null result: heatmaps don't change between steps

     Venue fit:
     COLM 2026 (perfect fit — reasoning + interpretability + multimodal), ACL,
     EMNLP, NeurIPS (workshop track minimum, main track if results are strong).
